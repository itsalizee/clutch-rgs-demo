# Target Aggregators & Studio Programs

How a new studio actually gets distributed, who to approach, and in what order.

> **Reality check.** Program names, owners, and terms change — this is a researched
> starting map, **not** a guarantee any program is open or a fit today. Verify current
> terms on each company's site and find the live contact on LinkedIn. Do **not** trust a
> stale email address; confirm the human first.

## How distribution works (the 30-second version)
You don't sell to operators one by one. You integrate **once** with an aggregator (a
content platform), and your games appear in the catalogue that hundreds of operators
already pull from. The aggregator handles operator relationships, the wallet, and often
compliance distribution. Most aggregators require your games to be **certified** and your
studio **licensed** (or to supply under their license) before real-money go-live — but
**technical integration and commercials start before that.**

## Tier 1 — Emerging-studio programs (START HERE)
These exist **specifically to onboard new studios** — they help with distribution and
sometimes certification/compliance. Best-fit for a pre-cert studio with a strong demo.

| Program | Why it fits you |
|---|---|
| **Pariplay — Ignite** | A dedicated program to take emerging studios to market: aggregation (Fusion), compliance, and distribution support. Purpose-built for exactly your stage. |
| **Relax Gaming — "Powered By" / Silver Bullet** | Onboards independent studios onto Relax's aggregation and operator network. Content-partner track for third-party studios. |
| **Yggdrasil — YGS Masters / GATI** | Franchise + tech-stack (GATI) that helps studios build to a pre-certified compliance standard and distribute. |
| **Swintt / Kalamba / other "partner studio" tracks** | Several mid-size suppliers run partner-studio programs — worth a scan for open onboarding. |

**Angle for Tier 1:** "New studio, two live provably-fair games on one engine, built to
certify — is your studio program open? Here's a link to play both right now."

## Tier 2 — Crypto-forward aggregators (fast, sandbox-friendly)
Often quicker to sandbox-integrate and more comfortable with a Curaçao path and crash/
instant content. Good for early momentum and crypto-casino distribution.

| Aggregator | Notes |
|---|---|
| **SOFTSWISS Game Aggregator** | Large, crypto-native ecosystem (home turf for crash/instant). Onboards third-party studios. |
| **Hub88 (EveryMatrix)** | Content aggregation, developer-friendly API, crypto-forward operator base. |
| **Slotegrator (APIgrator / GamesHub)** | Aggregates many newer studios; works with emerging providers; crypto-friendly. |
| **NuxGame / Turbo-adjacent hubs** | Turnkey + aggregation for crypto casinos; crash content is in demand. |

**Angle for Tier 2:** "Crash + a new step-multiplier title, server-authoritative and
provably fair, one integration. Your operators are asking for fresh crash — play it."

## Tier 3 — Large full-stack aggregators (broad reach, longer process)
Biggest operator reach, but more process and usually cert/license required up front.
Pursue in parallel; expect a longer cycle.

| Aggregator | Notes |
|---|---|
| **EveryMatrix (SlotMatrix)** | Huge catalogue + operator base. |
| **BetConstruct** | Full-stack platform + aggregation. |
| **Digitain / Delasport / Groove Gaming / Salsa Technology** | Broad aggregation; regional strengths. |

## Prioritization
1. **This week:** 3–4 Tier 1 studio programs (best structural fit) + 3 Tier 2 crypto
   aggregators (fastest to a sandbox).
2. **Next:** the rest of Tier 2.
3. **Parallel / slow-burn:** Tier 3 — start the conversation, expect quarters.

## Finding the right person (per target)
Search LinkedIn at the company for these titles, in order of usefulness:
- **Head of Game Onboarding / Content Onboarding**
- **Studio Partnerships / Content Partnerships**
- **Head of Content** / **Chief Product Officer**
- **Commercial Director / Head of Business Development**

Send a personalized note to **one** named person. A short LinkedIn connect + the play
link often beats a cold email. Log each in `pipeline-tracker.csv`.

## Also worth doing
- **Industry events** (ICE, SiGMA, SBC): studio programs and aggregators take pitches
  there; a demo you can hand someone on a phone is a huge advantage.
- **Directories** (SlotsCalendar/SlotCatalog list providers & aggregators) to sanity-
  check who aggregates crash/instant content and which operators want it.
