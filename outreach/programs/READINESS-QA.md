# Readiness Q&A — the questions a program will ask

Answer every one straight. For an emerging-studio program, **honesty + a playable,
differentiated product** is the winning combination — they know you're new; they're betting on
the games and the tech.

---

**"You're brand new — why should our program take you?"**
Because we're not pitching a concept — you can **play six finished, provably-fair games right
now**, including a differentiated flagship (Zenith). We've already built the hard part (a
server-authoritative RGS + real games); we need your certification and distribution. That's a
low-risk, high-upside partner for you.

**"What makes Zenith different from the 300+ crash/mines/crossing clones?"**
Same proven, addictive *core* (shared rising multiplier + one cash-out decision), but a
genuinely fresh *identity*: a 3D neon **vertical climb** — one climber, everyone in the round —
not a plane on a curve. It escapes crash's visual saturation while keeping its stickiness, and
it's engineered around the evidence-based engagement levers (shared "now," illusion of control,
fast cadence, clip-ready moments).

**"Is it certified? Licensed?"**
Not yet — and we won't claim otherwise. Our engine is **built to certify**: server-authoritative,
deterministic replay, append-only audit logs, an RTP harness, documented provably-fair scheme.
Certification and licensing are precisely what we're looking to your program to provide or
support.

**"Do we rebuild your games on our platform, or do you supply your own RGS?"**
Open to either. Our games are a thin client over a clean server-authoritative RGS with a
seamless-wallet contract, so porting onto your RGS/SDK is straightforward — or we supply under
your certification. Let's align on your preferred model.

**"What's your track record / volumes?"**
Zero — we're new, and we won't pretend otherwise. What we bring is finished product, verifiable
fairness, RTP that matches its configuration over large samples, and an engine built to your
compliance bar. We're pricing and positioning accordingly.

**"RTP and configurability?"**
Operator-configurable RTP (demo runs 97%); configured RTP equals realized RTP across large
samples — we can show the harness output. Players can also select volatility where the game
supports it (mine count, difficulty).

**"How do you prove fairness?"**
Live, in the demo (`/fairness`, `/fairness/cross`, `/fairness/vault`): the server seed is
committed as a published hash before the round and revealed after — anyone recomputes the
outcome. Crash adds player entropy + a public block-hash; mines/climb use commit-reveal
shuffles. Player-verifiable every round.

**"Responsible gaming?"**
Built into the engine (server-authoritative limits/controls; outcomes can't be manipulated
client-side). We'll map to your RG and jurisdictional requirements. We're candid that these
games are engaging by design, and we take player-protection seriously.

**"Team?"**
`[Answer honestly — who built this, any real background. If solo: "Independent founder; I built
the RGS and all six games." Do not invent a team.]`

**"Commercials — revenue share, exclusivity?"**
Standard content revenue-share, founding-partner terms, flexible to your model. Open to
discussing exclusivity/first-launch on Zenith. No hidden minimums.

**"Timeline to a live title?"**
Games are built now. Time-to-live is gated by certification + integration onto your platform —
your process drives that. We can move as fast as you can.

**"Which markets?"**
Fastest realistic path is **crypto-forward** first (where provably-fair is a marketed feature),
regulated markets (US/CA/AU/UK/EU) to follow as certification/licensing allows. Guided by which
operators your program unlocks.

1. **Play Zenith** — the flagship does more than any slide.
2. **One integration, six games, three mechanics, built-to-certify** — range + readiness.
3. **Honest status** — new studio, finished product, here for your cert + distribution.
