# Objection Handling — the hard questions, answered honestly

Aggregators have heard 500 pitches. These are the questions that decide the deal. Answer
straight — the honesty *is* the differentiator. Never invent a cert, a license, a volume,
or a logo.

---

**"Are your games certified (GLI / iTech)?"**
Not yet, and we won't claim it until they are — you can hold us to that. The engine is
built *to certify*: server-authoritative, deterministic replay, append-only audit logs,
and an RTP harness that proves configured RTP == realized RTP. Certification is in progress;
we're integrating with partners in parallel so we launch the day it clears. If it helps, we
can share the technical pack a lab will review.

**"Are you licensed as a B2B supplier?"**
In progress — Curaçao first, tier-1 (MGA/UKGC) to follow. We can also supply under a
partner's license where that model works. Let's talk about which path fits your network.

**"You're brand new — why should we take the risk?"**
Two reasons. One: you can *play the product right now* — it's real, not a deck. Two: our
whole architecture is one integration for a growing catalogue, so the cost of trying us is
low and the upside compounds with every title we add. Founding partners get the best terms
we'll ever offer.

**"What's your track record / volumes?"**
Zero — we're new, and we won't pretend otherwise. What we can show is the product, the
fairness math, the measured RTP distribution over 20M simulated rounds, and an engine built
to your compliance bar. We're asking you to bet on the product and the model, and we're
pricing accordingly.

**"How long is integration?"**
One REST + WebSocket + seamless-wallet contract. We build a per-aggregator adapter that maps
to *your* wallet API — debit/credit/rollback, idempotent by txId, integer minor units, with
auto-reconciliation. Sandbox first; both games ship on the same integration.

**"How do you prove fairness?"**
Live, in the demo: /fairness and /fairness/cross. The server seed is committed (a published
hash) before the round and revealed after — anyone can recompute the outcome. Crash adds
player entropy and a public Bitcoin block hash so outcomes can't be ground out in advance.
The player can verify every round; that's a retention story your operators can market.

**"What's the RTP and can operators configure it?"**
Yes — RTP is a per-operator parameter. Ascent runs up to 99% (the demo is at 97%); Ascent
Cross is 97% with player-selectable volatility. Configured RTP matches realized RTP across
large samples — we have the harness output to show it.

**"What about responsible gaming?"**
Built in, not bolted on — limits and controls live in the engine, and outcomes/timing are
server-side so they can't be manipulated client-side. Happy to map to your RG requirements.

**"Is it stable / can it scale?"**
Server-authoritative core, thin client, idempotent wallet, append-only logs. The demo runs
live today. Production hardening (Postgres-backed durable logs, HA) is on the path to the
cert deployment; we'll run the system the lab audits.

**"Why crash / why these two games?"**
Crash is the fastest-growing category — mobile-first, high-frequency, social — and most
catalogues have only one or two titles. Ascent is a strong crash entry; Ascent Cross is a
*mechanically different* step-multiplier climb that shares the same engine, so you get two
distinct player experiences for one integration. More titles ship on the same pipe.

**"What do you need from us?"**
A sandbox and a short technical spec of your wallet API, and alignment on commercials. We
do the adapter work. We can begin before certification completes so we're catalogue-ready
the moment it does.
